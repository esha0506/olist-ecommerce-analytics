# Executive Summary

**Olist E-Commerce Revenue & Funnel Intelligence**

Analysis of 96,096 customers and R$13.49M in product revenue across Olist's
2016-2018 transaction history reveals a business with strong operational
fundamentals but a critical structural gap in customer retention.

## The Headline Finding

Only **3.04% of customers ever make a second purchase**, and retention
collapses to under 1% within a single month for every customer cohort
measured — with no gradual decay curve. Olist's revenue has been built
almost entirely on new-customer acquisition, not repeat behavior.

## What's Working

- Revenue is broad-based across categories (top category = only 9.3% of
  revenue) — low concentration risk
- The order funnel is tight: 97.02% of orders reach successful delivery
- 90.37% of orders arrive *early*, averaging 12.9 days ahead of estimate

## What Needs Attention

- Delivery delay is the strongest driver of dissatisfaction in the dataset:
  review scores fall from 4.29 to 1.69 as delay increases past 8 days
- Lower-volume states show *higher* AOV (a -0.53 correlation between order
  volume and AOV across all 27 states) — a reach problem, not a demand
  problem
- High-value customers are mostly one-time big spenders, not loyal repeat
  buyers — the top 20% of customers by spend drive 56.67% of revenue, almost
  entirely through single large purchases

## Top 3 Recommendations

1. Target high-spend one-time buyers with second-purchase incentives, rather
   than building loyalty programs around a repeat-customer base too small to
   move revenue
2. Eliminate the long-tail of 8+ day delivery delays first — that's where
   satisfaction collapses most sharply
3. Invest in logistics/regional presence in high-AOV, low-volume states
   instead of regional discounting
